# Springer Capital — Amazon SES Compliance Audit
### Required Revisions: Privacy Policy · Terms of Service · Contact Form

> **Scope:** Every item below is a required revision. Nothing here is optional if you intend to pass AWS Trust & Safety review and maintain ongoing SES production access. Items are grouped by file/component.

---

## 1. Privacy Policy — `app/privacy/page.tsx`

### 1.1 Missing: Effective Date
**Current:** No date shown anywhere on the page.
**Required:** Add an "Effective Date" and "Last Updated" line at the very top of the policy body (e.g., `Effective: [Month] [Year] | Last Updated: [Month] [Year]`). AWS reviewers and regulators check this to confirm the policy is maintained and current.

---

### 1.2 Missing: Identity of the Data Controller / Company
**Current:** The policy refers to "Springer Capital" but never states the full legal name, registration jurisdiction, or physical mailing address of the entity responsible for data.
**Required:** Add a dedicated opening section that states:
- Full legal entity name (e.g., *Springer Capital LLC* or whatever the registered entity is)
- Registered jurisdiction (e.g., State of Illinois, USA)
- Physical mailing address — **430 Beverly Drive, Wilmette, Illinois 60091** (already on the Contact page; must be mirrored here)
- Primary contact email for privacy matters (e.g., `contact@springer.capital`)

---

### 1.3 Missing: Lawful Basis for Processing (GDPR — mandatory for EU/EEA data subjects)
**Current:** The policy states data is used to "provide, operate, and maintain" — with no lawful basis stated.
**Required:** Because Springer Capital operates in Hong Kong and has visitors from the EU/EEA, you must state the lawful basis for each processing activity:
- Contact form submissions → **Legitimate Interest / Contractual Necessity**
- Email follow-up communications → **Legitimate Interest** (with opt-out right)
- Analytics/cookies → **Consent**

Add a table or bulleted list mapping each data type to its lawful basis.

---

### 1.4 Incomplete: Data Retention Policy
**Current:** No mention of how long data is kept.
**Required:** State a concrete retention period for each data category. Example:
- Contact form data (name, email, phone, message): retained for **[X] months** after the inquiry is resolved, then deleted.
- Cookie/analytics data: retained per the cookie provider's default (state which provider and duration).
- Email correspondence: retained for **[X] years** for legal/business record purposes.

AWS SES reviewers expect you to demonstrate you are not retaining data indefinitely.

---

### 1.5 Missing: User Rights Section
**Current:** No mention of individual rights.
**Required:** Add an explicit section titled **"Your Rights"** covering all applicable rights depending on jurisdiction:
- **Right to Access** — request a copy of personal data held
- **Right to Rectification** — request correction of inaccurate data
- **Right to Erasure ("Right to be Forgotten")** — request deletion
- **Right to Restrict Processing**
- **Right to Data Portability**
- **Right to Object** to processing based on legitimate interest
- **Right to Withdraw Consent** at any time (for cookie-based or marketing communications)
- **CAN-SPAM / Opt-Out Right** — right to stop receiving commercial email within 10 business days of request
- State the mechanism to exercise these rights: email `contact@springer.capital` or equivalent

---

### 1.6 Missing: Email Communication and Opt-Out Mechanism
**Current:** The policy mentions communicating "for marketing and promotional purposes" but provides zero opt-out instruction.
**Required — Critical for SES:** Add a dedicated subsection under "How We Use Your Information" that states:
- By submitting the contact form, users may receive follow-up emails from Springer Capital
- **Every marketing or promotional email** will contain an unsubscribe link
- Opt-out requests will be honored within **10 business days** (CAN-SPAM requirement)
- Users may also opt out by emailing `contact@springer.capital` with "Unsubscribe" in the subject line
- Once opted out, the email address will be suppressed and will not receive future marketing communications

---

### 1.7 Missing: Cookie Policy Detail
**Current:** One paragraph mentioning "cookies and tracking technologies" with no specifics.
**Required:** Expand to include:
- **Types of cookies used:** Essential, Analytics, Performance (specify which — e.g., Google Analytics, or none)
- **Third-party cookies:** Name any third-party services that set cookies (e.g., Google Analytics, formsubmit.co which the form currently uses)
- **How to manage/refuse cookies:** Instruction to use browser settings or the cookie consent banner
- **Effect of refusing cookies:** State what breaks if cookies are declined
- Reference the cookie consent banner already implemented (`CookieBanner.tsx`) as the mechanism for managing consent

---

### 1.8 Missing: Third-Party Data Processors / Sub-Processors
**Current:** A vague mention of "third-party ad servers" — this is inaccurate; the site does not run ads.
**Required:** Remove the ad server reference. Replace with accurate disclosure of actual third-party processors:
- **formsubmit.co** — the current form `action` URL sends data to `greentree.group/Springer/thankyou.html` via an external form handler; this processor must be named and their data practices linked
- **Amazon Web Services (SES)** — if/when SES is used to send emails, name AWS as a processor and link to the [AWS Privacy Notice](https://aws.amazon.com/privacy/)
- **Analytics provider** — if any analytics tool is in use, name it
- State that Springer Capital does not sell personal data to third parties

---

### 1.9 Missing: International Data Transfer Disclosure
**Current:** No mention of cross-border data transfers.
**Required:** Because the company has offices in Hong Kong, China, and the US, and collects data from global visitors, you must disclose:
- Data may be transferred to and processed in the United States, Hong Kong, and/or China
- The legal basis or safeguards for such transfers (e.g., Standard Contractual Clauses for EU data, or adequacy decisions)
- For Hong Kong: compliance with the Personal Data (Privacy) Ordinance (PDPO) Cap. 486

---

### 1.10 Missing: Children's Privacy Statement
**Required:** A brief statement that the site is not directed at children under 13 (US) / under 16 (EU) and does not knowingly collect their data. This is a standard AWS/CAN-SPAM expectation.

---

### 1.11 Missing: Policy Update Notification
**Required:** State how users will be notified of material changes to the Privacy Policy (e.g., "We will update the Last Updated date and, for significant changes, notify users via email or a prominent notice on this page").

---

### 1.12 Missing: Contact / Complaints Section
**Required:** Add a final section stating:
- How to submit a privacy complaint or inquiry: `contact@springer.capital`
- Response timeframe: within **30 days**
- Right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority (e.g., ICO in UK, relevant EU DPA, or Hong Kong Privacy Commissioner for Personal Data)

## 3. Contact Form — `app/contact/page.tsx`

### 3.1 Missing: Consent / CTA Checkbox — **Required for SES**
**Current:** The form has Name, Email, Phone, Message, and a Submit button. There is no consent checkbox whatsoever.
**Required:** Add an **explicit opt-in checkbox** (unchecked by default) immediately above the Submit button with the following label text (adjust wording as needed):

```
☐  I agree to the Privacy Policy and consent to Springer Capital 
   contacting me regarding my inquiry. I understand I may withdraw 
   consent at any time by using the unsubscribe link in any email.
```

- The checkbox must be **unchecked by default** (no pre-ticking — this is a hard requirement under GDPR and CASL, and AWS evaluators check for this)
- The checkbox must be **required** (`required` attribute) — form cannot be submitted without it
- "Privacy Policy" must be a hyperlink to `/privacy`
- This constitutes the documented **express consent** record that Amazon SES requires you to be able to prove

---

### 3.2 Missing: Secondary Optional Marketing Opt-In
**Recommended for SES best practice:** Add a second, separate, **optional** (not required) checkbox below the mandatory consent checkbox:

```
☐  I would also like to receive occasional updates and insights 
   from Springer Capital. I can unsubscribe at any time.
```

This clearly separates transactional consent (responding to the inquiry) from marketing consent (future newsletters/updates), which is the gold standard AWS Trust & Safety looks for and what allows you to legally add users to a mailing list.

---

### 3.3 Issue: Form Action URL Points to External Domain
**Current:** `action="/thankyou"` with a hidden `_next` value pointing to `https://www.greentree.group/Springer/thankyou.html` — data is being sent through formsubmit.co or a similar handler at a third-party domain.
**Required for SES:** If you are using SES to send autoresponder/confirmation emails:
- The third-party form handler (`greentree.group`) must be named in the Privacy Policy as a data processor
- The form must display notice that data is processed by a third party (brief note below the form, e.g., *"Form submissions are securely processed by [FormHandler]. See our Privacy Policy."*)
- If transitioning to direct SES API integration, remove the `greentree.group` dependency and handle submissions server-side with documented bounce/complaint handling

---

### 3.4 Missing: Privacy Notice Near Form
**Required:** Add a brief inline note directly below the form fields (not just in the footer) that states:
> *By submitting this form, your information will be used to respond to your inquiry in accordance with our [Privacy Policy](/privacy).*

This is required by GDPR's transparency obligation at point of collection and is standard AWS SES review expectation.

## 4. Global Site Issues (Affect SES Review)

### 4.1 Footer: Privacy Policy Link Visibility
**Current:** Privacy Policy and Terms links exist in the footer but are small and easy to miss.
**Required:** AWS reviewers look for a clearly visible Privacy Policy link in the footer of every page. Ensure both links are present on every page, not just the homepage. Confirm the footer renders correctly on `/multi`, `/logistics`, `/hospitality`, `/development`, and `/mixed` pages.

---

### 4.2 Cookie Banner: Missing "Manage Preferences" Option
**Current:** The `CookieBanner.tsx` offers only "Accept All" or "Decline" — a binary choice.
**Required under GDPR:** Users must be able to **granularly manage cookie preferences** (at minimum: Essential vs. Analytics). This is not just a legal requirement — AWS reviewers increasingly check for it as evidence of a professional, compliant site. Add a "Manage Preferences" button that opens a modal with granular toggles.

---

### 4.3 Missing: `abuse@` or `postmaster@` Monitored Mailbox
**Required for SES production access:** AWS requires at least one monitored mailbox at your sending domain for abuse and postmaster emails. You must create and actively monitor:
- `abuse@springer.capital`
- `postmaster@springer.capital`

These must not bounce and must be checked regularly. Include this in your SES production access request.

---

### 4.4 Missing: Bounce and Complaint Handling Documentation
**Not on the website but required for SES:** Be prepared to document (in your SES production access request) how you handle:
- **Hard bounces:** immediate suppression, never retry
- **Spam complaints:** immediate suppression from all future sends
- **Soft bounces:** retry logic with a cap

You must implement AWS SNS notifications for bounce/complaint events and process them automatically or manually within 24–48 hours.
